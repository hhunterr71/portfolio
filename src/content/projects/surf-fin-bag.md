## Description

I went looking for a bag to hold my surf fins, wax, and spare parts between sessions, and couldn't find anything on the market that actually fit what I needed — so I designed and sewed one myself.

I had never sewn anything before this. Cutting, sewing, and assembling the bag was all new territory, but I've always found I can work through a problem I've never seen before by just breaking it down and figuring it out piece by piece. For a first iteration, the final product came out better than I expected.

## Design Criteria

- Hold two large single fins (~9")
- A dedicated, padded-feeling section for side bites (small fins)
- A safe spot for spare parts and a fin key
- Room for wax
- Waterproof
- Compact and easy to carry
- Stylish — something I'd actually want to bring to the beach, not just functional

## Materials and Methods

1. **Material:** X-Pac VX21 ("4G") waterproof fabric — durable, genuinely waterproof, and cheap and easy to source compared to most marine-grade fabrics.
2. **Construction:** hand-cut pattern pieces, sewn and assembled on a free sewing machine I picked up off Facebook Marketplace.
3. **Hardware:** zippers and webbing for the straps and compartment dividers.
4. **Cost:** about $15 total for zippers, webbing, and material.

## Next Steps

1. Refine the pattern for a second iteration now that the first one has proven the concept.
2. Make a second bag in a different colorway, since the pattern is dialed in.
