## Description

Our senior design team was tasked with researching and designing an affordable optical profilometer capable of measuring and digitizing the 2D surface topography of various sample materials.

**Deliverables:**
- Stay under a budget of $3,500.
- Accurately measure a sample to 100nm.
- Develop a working prototype.

To meet these requirements, we broke the project into several areas: optics, probe design, cantilever assembly, and the linear z-axis stage. After creating a chemically etched tungsten probe and fixing it to a small cantilever of spring steel, we had a means to physically interact with the substrate and begin developing a measurement method. The deflection of the cantilever is registered by a laser, reflected off the end of the spring steel onto a lateral-effect position sensor, which outputs varying voltages correlated to small distance shifts.

While I assisted with all parts of the project, my primary responsibility was the optics design — using an 850nm infrared laser passed through a neutral density filter to reduce intensity, then through a reversed Galilean beam expander to output a collimated beam with a reduced spot size.

Unfortunately, COVID was a major setback. We assembled the individual components of the optics assembly, stage, and cantilever assembly, but were unable to finalize the end product.
