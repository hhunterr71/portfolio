## Description

This was a personal project meant to help me fulfill a dream: traveling around the country to work while having unlimited access to the outdoor sports I've built my life around. It involved the research, design, and construction of a multi-purpose adventure vehicle that stuck within my initial budget constraints.

The most difficult part of a project like this is that it grows, changes, and ultimately becomes nothing like what you thought it would be at the start if you aren't careful. It's extremely important to stick to your original goals and not get distracted by what others have done. The van isn't meant to be perfect — it's a tool. As an engineer and a detail-oriented person, it was hard to tell myself when the project was "done," because tinkering and improving is fun.

### Key Highlights

- Designed and constructed a versatile adventure vehicle tailored to my specific needs and budget guidelines.
- Overcame challenges from project scope changes and evolving requirements, while staying true to original goals.
- Balanced perfectionism with functionality — the vehicle serves as a tool for exploration and adventure.
- Leveraged engineering expertise and attention to detail for optimal functionality and durability of the buildout.

## Design Constraints

**Must-haves for the vehicle:**
- Within budget
- Stealth (not conspicuously noticeable)
- Ability to stand inside
- Gear garage for bike, skis, etc.
- Reliability
- Easily drivable in the city

**Must-haves for the buildout:**
- Bed
- Power
- Gear garage
- Seating area
- Cooking area

## Lessons Learned

**Importance of goal adherence:** staying true to initial project objectives despite evolving visions and outside influence.

**Value of continuous improvement:** finding satisfaction in refining the build while still meeting completion criteria.

**Acknowledgment of oversight:** the van met my original criteria, but I later realized I needed 4WD for easy winter mountain access.

**Tailoring solutions to personal needs:** online recommendations don't always fit personal needs — my oversized electrical system cost extra but wasn't fully utilized. With 200W of solar and a 200Ah battery, my charge level never dropped below 80% even under heavy use; a 100Ah battery could have saved about $1,000.

These lessons sharpened my project management instincts and gave me invaluable insight for future builds.
