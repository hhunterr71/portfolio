Some films are born in a studio by someone with artistic vision. This one was born in a garage at 11pm with a warm tallboy and a heated debate about tire pressure. Nobody here has a film degree. Nobody asked us to make this. Four guys. Two cameras. Two corporate fugitives chasing something their 9-5 can't provide. A real "water bottle and a dream" kind of vibe.

A few rock skips off I-90 sits a small bike park called The Summit at Snoqualmie, and it doesn't get nearly the recognition it deserves as a tire-shredding, smile-bringing fantasy factory of debauchery and loam. Maintained by the kind of people who show up on weekdays with shovels just because they love the place. We aim to shed some light on the trails, the people, and the culture that exists there. We'll be picking up those shovels too, and likely making fools of ourselves in the process.

The vibe is simple: camaraderie, freedom, and riding as hard as we can into the sunset. The world is getting too serious, and this is our formal objection to that. Expect grunge music, slow motion that makes the dirt look like bonkers, and at least one guy crawling out of the bushes from an overcooked turn wondering where his bike went.

Filming this summer — check back for the real thing.
